Design & feature differences (short)

  • Tone: Draft 1 = premium/professional; Draft 2 = bold/technical; Draft 3 = friendly/local.
  • Visuals: 1 uses dark, desaturated photography; 2 is high-contrast with strong strokes; 3 uses warm accents, rounded cards.
  • Conversion focus: 2 highlights prices and direct booking distinctly; 1 emphasizes brand prestige and high-end quality; 3 emphasizes trust and locality (testimonials, simple booking card).
  • UX: All are single-page, mobile-responsive. Differences lie in CTA prominence, information density, and perceived price positioning.

Business analysis — how each option may affect Mike's business

Draft 1 — Premium positioning
  • Attracts higher-paying customers seeking premium results.
  • Best for differentiating from low-cost competitors; supports higher pricing and upsells.
  • May reduce volume of budget-conscious leads; needs high-quality photos and reviews to justify price.
Draft 2 — Direct conversion / tactical
  • Clear pricing and strong CTAs likely increase booking conversions and reduce friction.
  • Works well for busy owners who decide based on price and quick booking.
  • Risk: feels less personal — may lower perceived trust vs a local approach.
Draft 3 — Local / trust-driven
  • Builds rapport with local customers (community-first messaging).
  • Good for repeat business and referrals; emphasizes Mike as a person rather than a brand.
  • May convert slightly slower than Draft 2 but can increase CLTV (customer lifetime value).

Practical recommendations

  • If Mike wants to raise prices and target premium customers: pick Draft 1. Invest in high-quality photos and a short hero video; collect 5–10 strong before/after shots and 3–5 video clips.
  • If Mike wants more immediate bookings and simple messaging: pick Draft 2. Keep pricing visible and use a clear "Call or Text" CTA — consider adding a simple booking form or link to scheduling (Calendly/Turo style) in future.
  • If Mike prioritizes local trust, referrals, and repeat customers: pick Draft 3. Emphasize testimonials, neighborhoods served, and a personable About section. Offer a loyalty discount or referral credit to capture repeat business.
  • Hybrid option: combine Draft 2's clear booking/pricing with Draft 3's trust elements and Draft 1's premium photography — i.e., bold CTAs + personable copy + premium images.
