02 — Brand Decisions

You need to answer these before we go live

A website can't do its job until you've nailed these. I'll give you my suggestions, but these are your calls to make. Take your time with them because once you're on Google, changing a name or domain is a bigger deal than it seems.

Question 01 — Business Name

What do you want to be called?

The current placeholder is "Apex Detail Co." That's solid. It's strong, short, and searchable. But before we make it permanent, consider: does it feel like you? Is there something more specific to Ferndale or Whatcom County that could build local loyalty? Here are a few directions:

  • KEEP Apex Detail Co. Clean, professional, works everywhere. Strong with Draft 1 or 2.
  • LOCAL Whatcom Detail Co. Signals you're from here. Ranks well locally on Google.
  • LOCAL Ferndale Detail Hyper-local. Great for Nextdoor and community word of mouth.
  • PERSONAL Mike's Detail Co. If your whole brand is "Mike" — the guy, the story — this works great with Draft 3.
  • BOLD Apex Mobile Detail Emphasizes the mobile angle, which is a real differentiator in this market.
Question 02 — Slogan

What's the one sentence that defines you?

A slogan isn't marketing fluff. It's the fastest way to tell a stranger what makes you different. Right now the drafts use "Premium mobile detailing that transforms the ordinary into the extraordinary." That's fine but it could be sharper. Here are alternatives based on your actual strengths:

  • MOBILE "We come to you. No drop-off, no hassle." Simple and clear. Mobile is your biggest differentiator.
  • QUALITY "Your car, spotless. We come to you." Result-focused. Works well with before/after photos.
  • LOCAL "Whatcom County's detail guy." Owning the region. Personal and specific.
  • PREMIUM "Precision detailing. Right in your driveway." Premium feel, still emphasizes the mobile angle.
  • PUNCHY "We don't cut corners. Your car proves it." Quality-first, slightly bold. Fits Draft 1 or 2.
Question 03 — Domain Name

What's your web address going to be?

Your domain affects how easy it is to find you on Google and how easy it is to share by word of mouth. Short, specific, and memorable wins. I'd recommend checking these in order of preference. I can register and host any of these for you.

  • BEST apexdetailco.com Clean match to the business name. Easy to remember, easy to say.
  • GOOD apexdetail.com Shorter. Works if the full version is taken.
  • LOCAL whatcomdetail.com Strong local SEO signal. Ranks well for "Whatcom County detailing."
  • LOCAL ferndalemobiledetail.com Hyper-targeted. Excellent for Google local search.
  • OPTION apexdetailwhatcom.com Combines brand name and location. Good SEO, a bit long.
Question 04 — Your Story

What's the version of your story that actually connects?

Every draft has an "About" section but right now it's filler. The most effective thing you can do for your business online is tell a real story. People book Mike, not just a detail service. Consider answering these honestly and I'll write it up properly:

  • ASK Why did you start this? What pushed you to go out on your own? Real answers here build real trust.
  • ASK What do you do that most detail shops don't? Mobile, attention to detail, products you use, turnaround time?
  • ASK Who's your best customer? What does their car look like when you're done vs. when you started? This is the core of your testimonial strategy.
  • ASK What area do you actually cover? Are there towns you don't want to drive to? Being specific here is actually a trust signal.

Seasonal Patterns — Plan Your Marketing Around These

  • March / April: Spring pollen and tree sap season. PNW trees coat cars with sticky residue. Push "Spring Refresh" packages starting February.
  • May / June: Busy families prepping for summer road trips. Full detail demand peaks. Ceramic coating interest spikes for summer protection.
  • July / August: Summer population surge around Birch Bay. Short-season tourist traffic. Bug splatter and UV exposure bring people in.
  • September / October: Best time to push winter prep packages. Leaf tannin stains, early rain, and road grit create urgency. This is your upsell season.
  • November / February: Slowest period. Use it to lock in fleet contracts and collect reviews. Consider a "winter special" to keep cash flowing.

Where to Find Customers — Channel Priority

  • 1. Google Business Profile (GBP): Non-negotiable. Most "detailing near me" searches end here. You need photos, reviews, and weekly posts to rank. I'll set this up for you.
  • 2. Instagram: Before/after photos are the single best content type for detailing. You already have great before/afters. Start posting. Reels get reach for free.
  • 3. Nextdoor: Ferndale and Lynden are tight communities. Your neighbors trust recommendations here more than anywhere else. Create a free business page immediately.
  • 4. Facebook: Community groups and local Facebook pages are where Whatcom County families make service decisions. Worth maintaining a business page.
  • 5. Referral Program: Offer existing customers $20 off their next detail for every referral who books. Word of mouth is already working for you, this just accelerates it.

The real opportunity in this market

The Ferndale-to-Lynden corridor is underserved relative to Bellingham proper. Most competitors center their marketing in Bellingham. If you own "Ferndale, Lynden, Birch Bay" in local search results, you can carve out a defensible territory before the bigger players think to focus there. That's a real and achievable goal within six months of going live with a solid website and a Google Business Profile.

05 — My Recommendations

If I were making this call

Here's what I'd do, in plain terms. Take what's useful, push back on what doesn't feel right. That's what the conversation is for.

Launch with Draft 2, then evolve toward Draft 1

Draft 2 converts fastest. It gives customers what they need to make a decision: price, service, and a clear action. Start there. As your reviews build and your photos get stronger, you have the option to shift toward Draft 1's premium positioning and charge accordingly. Launching with Draft 1 before you have the reviews and photos to back it up is a risk.

Get 10 Google reviews before you launch the site

A website without reviews is a business without credibility. Before we push anything live, text your last 10 customers and ask them to leave a review. Give them the direct link. A business with 10 solid reviews outranks one with zero reviews almost every time, regardless of how good the website looks.

Own "Ferndale mobile detailing" in local search

The big competitors are fighting over "Bellingham detailing." You're in Ferndale. Make that your keyword territory. Your Google Business Profile, your website copy, and your Nextdoor presence should all reinforce that you're the Ferndale-area detailer. It's a smaller pond, but you can own it.

Add ceramic coating as a service within 90 days

If you are interested in this it's a great option. This is the highest-margin thing you can offer. Even one job per week at $800 adds over $40,000 a year to your revenue. The Bellingham market is actively searching for it. If you can get certified and set up a clean environment to apply it, this changes your business model faster than anything else.

Lock in a fleet client in the first 120 days

One contractor with four trucks that you detail twice a month is $800–$1,200 in predictable income. That's your floor. Reach out to local contractors, tradespeople, and small businesses in the Ferndale/Lynden area. They hate managing dirty work vehicles and most of them don't have a current solution.
