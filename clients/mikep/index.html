01 — The Three Drafts

Three directions, three different customers

Each draft targeted a different version of the ideal client. The design isn't decoration. It's a signal. Here's what each one communicated before anyone read a single word.

Draft 1
The Premium Play
Dark, gold, cinematic. Luxury at a glance.
  • Attracts customers who judge quality before price
  • Sets up higher price points without needing to justify them
  • Custom cursor, film-grain texture, editorial feel
  • Best fit: Tesla owners, newer trucks, high-end imports
View Draft 1
Draft 2
The Direct Closer
Red, black, bold. Built to convert fast.
  • Price front and center; no ambiguity
  • Strong CTAs and tight layout drives bookings
  • Feels like a serious, professional operation
  • Best fit: busy customers who decide on price and convenience
View Draft 2
Draft 3
The Local Favorite
Yellow, warm, approachable. Mike-as-a-person.
  • You feel like a neighbor, not a service provider
  • Builds trust and repeat business over time
  • Testimonials and personality carry this design
  • Best fit: families, neighbors, Nextdoor referrals
View Draft 3
Draft 4 — The Hybrid
Draft 2 layout. Draft 1 color.
The bold structure and clear CTAs from Draft 2, repainted in the gold and black palette from Draft 1. This iteration led directly to the chosen direction.

Why we landed on the hybrid

Draft 2 converts fastest — price up front, clear action, no friction. Draft 1 feels premium — gold, dark, editorial. Neither alone was quite right. Combining the layout of Draft 2 with the color system of Draft 1 gave us something that does both: it converts like a direct-response page but reads like a premium brand. That's Draft 4, now Pinnacle Auto Detail.
